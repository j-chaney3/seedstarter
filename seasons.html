When should I plant?

The best time to start your seeds for your garden depends on several factors, such as the type of plant, your climate, and the expected planting date. In general, it is recommended to start seeds indoors 4-12 weeks before the last expected frost in your area.If you are unsure about the last expected frost date, you can check with your local gardening center or use an online tool to find your frost dates based on your zip code.Some plants, like tomatoes, peppers, and eggplants, require a longer growing season, and it's recommended to start their seeds earlier. Other plants, like cucumbers, beans, and squash, grow more quickly and can be started a little later.

Source: USDA

It's important to follow the instructions on the seed packet for each specific plant, as they will provide information on when to start seeds and how to care for them. Refer to the US garden zone map to compare your specific zone to the seeds you are starting.
